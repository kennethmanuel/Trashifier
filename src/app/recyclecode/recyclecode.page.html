RECYCLING CODE

Recycling codes, are symbols or numbers used to classify different types of materials for recycling purposes. While they are commonly associated with plastics, they can extend beyond plastics to include other recyclable materials such as paper, glass, and metals. These codes help identify and sort materials to facilitate the recycling process.

Note that the presence on an item of a recycling code, a chasing arrows logo, or a resin code, is not an automatic indicator that a material is recyclable; it is an explanation of what the item is made of.

The first seventh of the recycling codes are called Resin Identification Code. The resin identification codes for plastics consist of a number from 1 to 7, surrounded by the recycling symbol.

Each number represents a specific type of plastic resin, such as PET (polyethylene terephthalate), HDPE (high-density polyethylene), PVC (polyvinyl chloride), LDPE (low-density polyethylene), PP (polypropylene), PS (polystyrene), and others. These codes help recycling facilities sort and process plastic waste more efficiently.
